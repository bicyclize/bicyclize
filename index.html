Why bicyclize?

Our transport systems are challenged by today's rapid urbanisation. According to the UN, the world urban population is expected to increase further by 72 per cent by 2050. By mid-century the world urban population will likely be the same size as the world’s total population was in 2002.

Cycling as transport

  • Automated vehicles
  • Efficiency
  • Speed
  • Social equity
  • Freedom

While automated vehicles will substantially improve the space demand of current motorisation, the safety measures in automated vehicles will also improve percieved cycling and pedestrian safety, further improving attractiveness to cycling

Furthermore the smooth flow of interconnected automated vehicles will require the elimination of road users that are not communicating with them, eg. pedestirans and cyclists. There is a strong incentive to increase the number of attractive segregated cycle lanes, to move cycling to alternative routes, resulting on smoother automated vehicle flow, but consequently attracting more cycling.

Proper cycling infrastructure provision benefits all transport modes easing the pressure from public transit and motorised road use. Attracting drivers to cycle shorter trips means less congestion for those who are less flexible in chosing alternative modes.

Cycling is fastest mode of transport mode in many congested cities. Beside the direct journey speed, if one consideres the time spent on purchasing access i.e. a bicycle vs vehicle, the time saved on not needing to spend extensive amounts on transport puts cycling in the very top.

While public funding finances transport infrastructure the individual may only benefit from investment if uses it. In the UK, a heavily motorised society, less than half of the nation drives and less than a quarter does daily more than 10km. Consequently the majority of population subsidise these beneficiaries.

Cycling infrastructure requires no substansive additional investment from individual.

Cycling offers the flexibility to travel without any schedule and with no additional costs.

What we do
We devote our expertise to make cities SMART and LIVEABLE. We collect urban movement data from installed and public CCTV cameras, with the aims to improve cycling and walking conditions.
  • Imagery analysis
    Based on Segnet's Deep Convolutional Architecture we transform imagery data into numbers that represent the volume of traffic in a given place according to transport mode.
  • Visualising mobility
    We are working closely with our partner Fileradar BV to feed this new data to Dantesoftware to provide valuable insights at micro and macro level for merchants and municipalities to understand traffic flow.
Business

The micro level result of our data collection allows us to provide specific traffic information for merchants and local service providers to target their audience.

Municipal clients

Our hollistic approach allows us to provide macro level advisory on transport policy to municipal stake holders.

Data collection

May it be radar detectors or imagery data source, we are prepared to turn raw data into quantifiable information.

Analytics and forecasting

Should you requiure GIS or dynamic mapping data visualisation we are ready with Fileradar's Dantesoftware to analyse and provide forecasting for the entire transport network.
